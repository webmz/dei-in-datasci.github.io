Adolescent Brain Cognitive Development (ABCD) Data Repository

The ABCD Data Repository houses all data generated by the ABCD Study, a collaborative effort sponsored by several NIH Institutes, which tracked the biological and behavioral development fo 10,000 children through their adolesense (ages 9-10) into young adulthood. The repsoitory is a part of the National Institute of Mental Health Data Archive (NDA), an informatics platform that facilitates data sharing across all of mental health and other research communities.

Qualified researchers can request access to ABCD shared data here.

Data types:

  • Minimally processed brain image volumes
  • Tabulated structural MRI, diffusion MRI, resting-state fMRI and task fMRI results
  • Non-imaging assessment data from the physical & mental health, neurocognition, substance use, biospecimens and culture & environment domains

GenBank

GenBank is the NIH genetic sequence database, an annotated collection of all publicly available DNA sequences. GenBank is part of the International Nucleotide Sequence Database Collaboration. GenBank is one of several NCBI databases, which collectively provide access to biomedical and genomic information.

There are several ways to search and retrieve data from GenBank.

  • Search GenBank for sequence identifiers and annotations with Entrez Nucleotide.
  • Search and align GenBank sequences to a query sequence using BLAST (Basic Local Alignment Search Tool). See BLAST info for more information about the numerous BLAST databases.
  • Search, link, and download sequences programatically using NCBI e-utilities. The ASN.1 and flatfile formats are available at NCBI's anonymous FTP server.

Data types: RNA or genomic sequence data, including expressed sequence tags (ESTs), complete microbial genomes, whole genome shotgun (WGS) sequences, and more

Human Connectome Project

The overarching purpose of the Human Connectome Project is to acquire and share data about the structural and functional connectivity of the human brain, while advancing the capabilities for imaging and analyzing brain connections.

The NIH has funded two consortium sites to carry out the HCP. Washington University in St. Louis and the University of Minnesota lead a consortium aiming to comprehensively map long-distance brain connections and their variability through neuroimaging of 1,200 healthy adults (twins and their non-twin siblings). The Massachusetts General Hospital and UCLA consortium has built a next-generation 3T magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scanner to translate the resulting data into connectomic maps detailing the fibrous connections in the brain.

Data type: Neuroimaging data

ImmPort

ImmPort Shared Data enables searching and downloading shared biomedical research data funded from NIAID Division of Allergy, Immunology & Transplantation and Division of Microbiology & Infectious Diseases, other NIH agencies, and non-government sources.

Data types:

  • RNA and DNA sequencing data
  • Mass spectrometry data
  • Microarray data
  • Microscopy data

National Cancer Institute Genomic Data Commons

The GDC Data Portal is a robust data-driven platform that allows cancer researchers and bioinformaticians to search and download cancer data for analysis. It includes datasets generated from several projects, including:

  • The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) has generated comprehensive, multi-dimensional maps of the key genomic changes in 33 types of cancer. The TCGA dataset is made up of 2.5 petabytes of data describing tumor tissue and matched normal tissues from more than 11,000 patients.
  • Therapeutically Applicable Research To Generate Effective Treatments (TARGET), which applies a comprehensive genomic approach to determine molecular changes that drive childhood cancers.

Data types:

  • DNA and RNA sequencing data, in both FASTQ and BAM formats
  • Clinical data, including demographic, diagnosis, exposure, and family history information
  • Microscopy data

National Database for Autism Research

The National Database for Autism Research (NDAR) is an NIH-funded data repository that aims to accelerate progress in autism spectrum disorder (ASD) research. NDAR is one of several databases that make up the National Institute of Mental Health Data Archive (NDA). NDAR accepts, houses, and shares with qualified researchers, harmonized, item-level, ASD-related data of all types, and concerning all levels of biological and behavioral organization.

Data types:

  • Standard phenotypic data
  • Descriptive data (demographics, summary subject measures, diagnostic measures)
  • Imaging data
  • Genomic sequencing data

PubMed

PubMed comprises more than 28 million citations for biomedical literature from MEDLINE, life science journals, and online books. Citations may include links to full-text content from PubMed Central and publisher web sites.

Data types: Citations

PubMed Central (PMC)

PubMed Central (PMC) is a free full-text archive of biomedical and life sciences journal literature at the National Library of Medicine. In addition to its role as an archive, the value of PMC lies in its capacity to store and cross-reference data from diverse sources using a common format within a single repository. With PMC, a user can quickly search the entire collection of full-text articles and locate all relevant material. PMC also allows for the integration of its literature with a variety of other information resources that can enhance the research and knowledge fields of scientists, clinicians and others.

Data types: Text manuscripts
